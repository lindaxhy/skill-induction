Compute is growing considerably faster than web text available for language model pre-training, yet synthetic data augmentation remains underexplored for modeling the original web distribution rather than transforming data into higher-quality or task-specific formats. We operate under the asymptotically infinite compute regime, training the best possible 300M parameter model given 200M real tokens under unlimited compute. We first show that mixing rephrases with real data improves i.i.d. validation loss despite the synthetic data coming from a different distribution, with loss plateauing at 3.41 at 32 rephrases versus a real-data-only baseline of 3.55, corresponding to a 1.48× data efficiency improvement and a 5% average downstream accuracy gain. We then introduce the perspective that synthetic generations should form a single long megadocument rather than being shuffled as independent short documents, and propose two instantiations: stitched rephrasing and latent thoughts. Both megadoc methods outperform simple rephrasing on i.i.d. loss, long-context loss, and downstream benchmarks, with stitched rephrasing and latent thoughts achieving 1.64× and 1.80× data efficiency at 32 generations and showing less plateauing under generation count, improving downstream accuracy by 6% and 9% over the real-data-only baseline. Finally, all three synthetic data methods compose with ensembling, while self-distillation does not, demonstrating a qualitatively unique benefit of the megadocument algorithms introduced in this paper.
