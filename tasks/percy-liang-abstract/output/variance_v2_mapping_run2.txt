Compute is growing considerably faster than web text available for language model pre-training, yet synthetic data augmentation has been explored primarily for format and quality transformation rather than for modeling the original web distribution. We address this gap under the asymptotically infinite compute regime, training 300M parameter models on 200M real tokens with an external instruction-tuned generator. We first show that mixing real data with rephrases improves i.i.d. validation loss monotonically up to 32 rephrases per document, plateauing at 3.41 versus a real-data-only baseline of 3.55, corresponding to 1.48× data efficiency and a 5% downstream accuracy improvement. We then introduce the megadocument perspective, which treats synthetic generations as a single long document rather than shuffled independent short documents, and propose two instantiations: stitched rephrasing, which concatenates rephrases from the same source document, and latent thoughts, which inserts rationales connecting chunks of each document. Both megadocument methods outperform simple rephrasing on i.i.d. loss, long-context loss, and downstream benchmarks, achieving 1.64× and 1.80× data efficiency at 32 generations versus 1.48× for simple rephrasing, with less plateauing and 6% and 9% downstream accuracy gains. Finally, all three synthetic data methods compose with the data-efficient strategy of ensembling, while self-distillation does not, demonstrating a qualitatively distinct benefit of scaling synthetic megadocs.
