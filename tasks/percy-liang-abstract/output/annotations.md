### Paper 1 — *Replaying pre-training data improves fine-tuning*

This abstract strongly uses **Mechanism 1: standard-practice inversion**, first stating the norm — “**Typically, generic data is only mixed in during fine-tuning to prevent catastrophic forgetting of the generic domain**” — and then overturning it with “**We surprisingly find that replaying the generic data during fine-tuning can actually improve performance on the (less related) target task**.”  
It also uses **Mechanism 4: scale tuple inserted mid-abstract** and **Mechanism 3: data-efficiency as common currency** in “**Concretely, in a controlled pre-training environment with 4M target tokens, 4B total tokens, and 150M parameter models, generic replay increases target data efficiency by up to \(1.87\times\) for fine-tuning and \(2.06\times\) for mid-training**.”  
Finally, it shows **Mechanism 5: objective-first, benchmark-last bridge** with “**We demonstrate the success of replay in practice for fine-tuning 8B parameter models, improving agentic web navigation success by \(4.5\%\) and Basque question-answering accuracy by \(2\%\)**.”  
Structurally, it matches the fingerprint’s regime-first opening (“**To obtain… the current paradigm is…**”), uses discourse markers like “**Typically,**” “**Concretely,**” and “**We further analyze**,” and relies on empirical verbs/nouns such as **find, analyze, demonstrate, current paradigm, fine-tuning, mid-training, target task,** and **data efficiency**.

### Paper 2 — *Pre-training under infinite compute*

This abstract most clearly exhibits **Mechanism 2: asymptote-as-evaluation target**, first in “**we estimate its best possible performance via the asymptote of its scaling law rather than the performance at a fixed compute budget**” and then in “**We then identify that ensembling independently trained models achieves a significantly lower loss asymptote than the regularized recipe**.”  
It also uses **Mechanism 3: data-efficiency as common currency** plus a lighter **Mechanism 4: scale insertion**, e.g. “**achieves an asymptote at 200M tokens using \(5.17\times\) less data than our baseline**,” “**a student model that is \(8\times\) smaller and retains \(83\%\) of the ensembling benefit**,” and “**a \(17.5\times\) data efficiency improvement**.”  
The abstract also has **Mechanism 5: objective-first, benchmark-last bridge** in “**Finally, our interventions designed for validation loss generalize to downstream benchmarks**,” after building the case through loss, scaling laws, and asymptotes; it additionally includes a standard-practice correction in “**the optimal weight decay is \(30\times\) larger than standard practice**.”  
Its structure closely follows the fingerprint: a macro-trend opening (“**Since compute grows much faster than web text…**”), clean progression via “**We first show… We then identify… Finally… Our results show…**,” and signature vocabulary such as **fixed data, no compute constraints, existing data-constrained approaches, regularized recipe, scaling law, loss asymptote, baseline,** and **student model**.
